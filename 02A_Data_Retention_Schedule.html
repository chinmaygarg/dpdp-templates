DPDP Act 2023 Reference: Section 8(7) — A Data Fiduciary shall, unless retention is necessary for legal purposes, delete personal data as soon as the purpose for which it was collected is no longer being served. This schedule maps every data category to its retention period, legal basis, and deletion trigger. Templates #2 (Governance) and #31 (Operations) in the DPDP Compliance Template Library both reference this document.
IMPORTANT — Statutory Overrides: Where applicable law (PMLA, RBI, IRDAI, SEBI, Income Tax Act) mandates a retention period longer than the purpose-completion period, the statutory period takes precedence. Consult the Statutory Hold Register (Template #43) before scheduling any deletion.

SECTION 5 — DELETION TRIGGER DECISION FLOWCHART
Before scheduling any deletion, validate all three conditions:
  1. Purpose Check: Has the original purpose for which data was collected been fully served? (Section 8(7))
  2. Statutory Hold Check: Does any applicable law (PMLA, RBI, IRDAI, SEBI, Income Tax) require retention beyond the purpose-completion date? Refer to Statutory Hold Register.
  3. Consent Check: If consent-based, has consent been withdrawn? If yes, initiate deletion unless statutory hold applies.
If all conditions clear → proceed to deletion → log in Deletion Log (Template #32) → notify processors via Processor Deletion Confirmation (Template #33).
