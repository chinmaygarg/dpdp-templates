Scope & Purpose: This Policy governs how ("the Organization") collects, uses, stores, and deletes personal data of its employees, contractors, interns, and former employees in compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 ("DPDP Act"). This Policy is distinct from the Employee Data Protection Training Programme (Template #19) and from the general Data Protection Policy (Template #18). It is to be read alongside the Data Retention Schedule and the Master Data Protection Policy.

SECTION 2 — EMPLOYEE RIGHTS UNDER DPDP ACT 2023
All employees have the following rights under the DPDP Act 2023. Requests must be submitted to the DPO at:
Right DPDP Section What the Employee Can Request Organization's Response SLA Limitations / Exceptions
Right to Access (Summary) Section 11 Summary of personal data held; list of processors to whom data was shared Acknowledge within 7 days; respond within 30 days Legal privilege / ongoing investigation data may be withheld
Right to Correction Section 12 Correct inaccurate or outdated personal data (e.g., address, contact, name change) Update within 15 business days of verification Payroll data changes follow payroll cycle cut-off dates
Right to Erasure Section 12 Request deletion of personal data no longer necessary for employment purposes Assess within 30 days; delete or explain statutory hold within 90 days Statutory obligations (PF, ESI, Income Tax) override erasure; cannot delete payroll records during mandatory hold
Right to Nominate Section 14 Nominate a family member or trusted person to exercise rights in case of death or incapacity Register nominee within 15 days of request Nominee must provide identity proof; scope of access defined by employee
Right to Grievance Section 13 Raise complaint about data processing; escalate to Data Protection Board if unresolved DPO responds within 30 days; Board escalation available after 30 days —
Right to Withdraw Consent Section 6 Withdraw consent for discretionary processing (e.g., voluntary health info, optional HR analytics) Cease processing within 7 days of withdrawal request Withdrawal does not affect lawfulness of processing before withdrawal; cannot withdraw for statutory / legitimate use data

SECTION 5 — DATA PROTECTION IN JOB DESCRIPTIONS

The following roles MUST include explicit data protection and confidentiality clauses in their job descriptions, onboarding documentation, and annual role confirmations. These roles either: (a) handle sensitive personal data, (b) have elevated system access, or (c) make decisions affecting data principals. Failure to include data protection clauses in the JD for these roles is a compliance gap (SS-04 checkpoint).

SECTION 6 — POLICY COMPLIANCE OBLIGATIONS FOR EMPLOYEES
5.1 Obligation to Maintain Confidentiality: All employees who handle personal data of colleagues, customers, or any other individuals must maintain strict confidentiality. Unauthorized access, disclosure, or sharing of personal data is a disciplinary offence and may result in termination and/or legal action.
5.2 Data Handling in Job Duties: Employees must handle personal data only for the purposes for which it was provided, using approved systems, and must not transfer data to personal devices or unsanctioned cloud storage. Refer to the IT Acceptable Use Policy for system-specific rules.
5.3 Breach Reporting Duty: Any employee who suspects or discovers a personal data breach (accidental loss, unauthorized access, disclosure) must report it immediately to the DPO at or the IT Security team. The organization is required to notify the Data Protection Board within 72 hours of a breach (Section 8(6)).
5.4 Training Requirement: All employees must complete the mandatory DPDP awareness training within days of joining and annually thereafter. Refer to Template #19 — Employee Training Programme for curriculum details.
5.5 Consequences of Non-Compliance: Breach of this Policy may result in: (1) Formal warning, (2) Suspension pending investigation, (3) Termination with cause, (4) Civil liability under the DPDP Act 2023 (penalties up to ₹250 crore per breach event for the organization; individual liability per applicable law).
