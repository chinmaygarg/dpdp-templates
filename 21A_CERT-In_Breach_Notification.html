CERT-In Breach Notification Form (CC-5)
6-Hour Incident Reporting to Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In 2022)
Version: 1.0 (NEW) | Effective Date: 13 May 2027 | Status: Active

Overview

Under the CERT-In Direction 2022, all organizations in India must report cybersecurity incidents to CERT-In within 6 hours of detection. This is separate and independent from DPDP breach notification. CERT-In is the primary cybersecurity regulator and covers ALL incidents (data breach, ransomware, intrusion, DDoS, malware, etc.), even if no personal data is involved.

⚠️ CRITICAL: Many organizations report only to DPBI (72-hour personal data breach SLA) and ignore CERT-In. This is a separate, mandatory requirement with a tighter 6-hour deadline.

CERT-In Authority & Scope

What is CERT-In?

CERT-In (Indian Computer Emergency Response Team) is the statutory cybersecurity incident coordination body under the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology (MeitY). CERT-In:

  • Monitors cybersecurity threats to India's critical information infrastructure
  • Coordinates incident response and threat intelligence
  • Issues advisories and security alerts
  • Enforces the CERT-In Direction 2022 (mandatory incident reporting)

CERT-In Direction 2022 — Key Requirements

📋 MANDATORY REPORTING:
• Incident Types: Data breaches, ransomware, intrusions, DDoS, malware, website defacement, phishing, etc.
• SLA: Within 6 hours of detection
• Form: CC-5 (Incident Report Form)
• Submission: incidents@cert-in.org.in
• Penalties for Non-Compliance: Up to ₹5 Lakhs or 2 years imprisonment (ITA 2000 Section 70)

Difference: CERT-In (6h) vs DPBI (72h)

Aspect CERT-In DPBI
Authority Ministry of Electronics & IT (MeitY) Data Protection Board of India
Scope ALL cybersecurity incidents (breach, DDoS, ransomware, malware, etc.) Only incidents involving personal data breach
SLA 6 hours of detection 72 hours of detection
Purpose Cybersecurity threat intelligence & coordination Data protection compliance enforcement
Form CC-5 DPBI Breach Notification Form (T-21)
Enforcement ITA 2000 Section 70; up to ₹5 Lakhs + 2yr prison DPDP Act 2023 Section 25; up to ₹50 Cr or 2% revenue

Example Scenarios

  • 🔴 DDoS attack (no data breach): Report to CERT-In (6hr) — NOT to DPBI (no personal data)
  • 🔴 Ransomware (no data accessed, just encryption): Report to CERT-In (6hr) — possibly DPBI if systems contain personal data
  • 🔴 Data exfiltration of customer PII: Report to BOTH CERT-In (6hr) AND DPBI (72hr)
  • 🔴 Phishing campaign (failed, no compromise): Report to CERT-In (6hr) — NOT to DPBI (no data loss)

6-Hour Reporting Timeline

The 6-hour clock starts from detection of the incident, not when the organization realizes the severity.

Time Action Deadline
T+0 (Hour 0) Incident detected (alert, log, report, etc.) Incident declared
T+1 (Hour 1) Incident Commander activated; initial classification Classification locked (type + severity)
T+2-5 (Hours 2-5) Prepare CC-5 form; gather systems affected, attack vector, initial containment steps Form prepared & ready to submit
T+6 (Hour 6) 🔴 CC-5 Form SUBMITTED to incidents@cert-in.org.in MANDATORY DEADLINE
✅ BEST PRACTICE:
Set an automatic alarm/reminder at T+5 hours to ensure submission before 6-hour deadline. Create a shared incident tracker that flags CERT-In deadline prominently.

Reportable Incident Types

ALL of the following must be reported to CERT-In within 6 hours:

Incident Type Description Example
Data Breach Unauthorized access and disclosure of sensitive data Attacker exfiltrates customer PII from database
Ransomware Attack Malware that encrypts files and demands ransom LockBit ransomware encrypts all servers; ransom note appears
Unauthorized Access Attacker gains access to systems without permission Compromised SSH key allows remote login to production server
DDoS (Distributed Denial of Service) Massive traffic flood to overwhelm service Website receives 1M requests/second; goes offline
Malware Infection Virus, worm, trojan, or other malicious code Emotet banking trojan steals credentials from endpoint
Website Defacement Attacker alters website content Company homepage replaced with hacker message
Phishing / Social Engineering Deceptive communication to compromise systems Spear phishing email tricks employee into installing backdoor
Insider Threat Employee or contractor intentionally causes harm System admin copies database to USB and leaves organization
Configuration Error / Misconfiguration Unintended exposure of sensitive systems/data AWS S3 bucket configured public; millions of records exposed
Zero-Day Exploitation Attack using previously unknown vulnerability Attacker exploits unpatched Apache vulnerability to gain RCE

How to Submit

Submission Instructions

  1. Email Address: incidents@cert-in.org.in
  2. Email Subject Line: Use format: Incident Report — [INCIDENT_ID] — [Type] — [Severity]
    Example: "Incident Report — INC-2026-04-15-001 — Ransomware — Critical"
  3. Email Body: Paste the completed CC-5 form in the email body (not as attachment)
  4. Attachments: Attach logs, evidence, forensics in a password-protected ZIP or encrypted email
  5. Confidentiality Note: Mark email as "CONFIDENTIAL" to request CERT-In not disclose details to third parties
  6. Proof of Submission: CERT-In will send acknowledgement email within 24 hours. Retain for audit trail.

Submission Timeline Checklist

  • ☐ T+2hr: Form CC-5 draft completed with available information
  • ☐ T+4hr: Evidence collected (logs, screenshots, network details)
  • ☐ T+5.5hr: Form reviewed by CISO; final check for completeness
  • ☐ T+5.55hr: Email composed and tested (test send to internal address first)
  • ☐ T+5.58hr: Final submission sent to incidents@cert-in.org.in
  • ☐ T+6hr: Deadline met; save email confirmation for audit trail

CERT-In Follow-up & Escalation

What to Expect After Submission

  • Acknowledgement (within 24 hours): CERT-In sends email confirming receipt of incident report
  • Additional Information Request (within 2-3 days): CERT-In may request more details, logs, or clarifications
  • Threat Intelligence Sharing (ongoing): CERT-In may provide IOCs (IP addresses, domains, malware hashes) to help with defense
  • Incident Tracker: CERT-In may create internal incident tracker and request periodic updates
  • Advisory Issuance (3-30 days post-incident): CERT-In may issue public security advisory if threat is widespread

Non-Compliance Penalties

⚠️ PENALTIES FOR MISSING CERT-In 6-HOUR SLA:
• Legal Basis: Information Technology Act 2000, Section 70
• Penalty: Up to ₹5 Lakhs (₹500,000) or 2 years imprisonment
• Enforcement: Can be enforced even if DPDP penalties also apply

Implementation Checklist

  • ☐ Policy: CERT-In 6-hour reporting requirement documented in Incident Response Policy
  • ☐ Team Training: All incident responders trained on CERT-In requirements, 6-hour SLA, Form CC-5
  • ☐ Email Account: Dedicated email account for CERT-In submissions (monitored by multiple people)
  • ☐ Incident Classification: Procedure to classify incidents as CERT-In-reportable within 1 hour of detection
  • ☐ T+5hr Alarm: Automated reminder / calendar alert at T+5 hours to submit before deadline
  • ☐ CC-5 Template: Blank CC-5 form template stored in shared incident response wiki/runbook
  • ☐ Evidence Collection SOP: Procedure to collect logs, screenshots, evidence within 2-4 hours of detection
  • ☐ CISO Sign-off: CISO (or designated authority) reviews CC-5 form before submission
  • ☐ Submission Tracker: Log of all CERT-In submissions (date, time, incident ID, form file, confirmation receipt)
  • ☐ Follow-up Response: Procedure to respond to CERT-In additional information requests within 2 business days
  • ☐ Annual Tabletop: Incident response drill includes CERT-In 6-hour reporting scenario (test email submission)
  • ☐ Cross-Reference: Link T-20 (Incident Response Plan) to T-21A (this template) in both docs
