17-Domain DPDP Compliance Checklist

How to Use: This checklist maps all DPDP compliance requirements to 17 distinct domains. For each domain, verify compliance status and document evidence collected. The consultant signs off when all mandatory criteria are met.
Domain DPDP Section Mandatory? Evidence Template Status

Governance & Certification Templates (Phase 4-5)

  • 39. Privacy Committee Charter — Committee membership, meeting cadence, decision authority
  • 40. Board Resolution — DPDP Compliance — Formal board acknowledgement of compliance program
  • 41. Compliance Certificate / Opinion Letter — Formal compliance opinion from consultant
  • 42. Consent Audit Worksheet — Sample-based audit of consent records against FSIUU criteria
  • 43. Statutory Hold Register — Data that cannot be deleted due to legal obligation (RBI, PMLA, etc.)
  • 44. Cross-Border Transfer Register — Destination countries, legal basis, contractual safeguards
  • 45. Ongoing Obligations Calendar — Annual review triggers, re-consent cycles, SDF audit schedule
  • 46. DPIA Register — Log of all DPIAs with status and next review date (SDF only)
  • 47. Compliance Status Report (Board Pack) — Executive summary for board: domain scores, open risks, certifications

Gap-Filled Templates (Added to complete 40-template MD index coverage)

  • 2A. Data Retention Schedule — Per-data-category retention periods, legal basis, deletion triggers, statutory overrides, and annual review log (covers MD Templates #2 & #31)
  • 3A. Employee Data Protection Policy — How employee personal data is processed: purpose per data category, retention periods, employee rights, consent register, third-party sharing, and policy compliance obligations (distinct from Training Template #19)
  • 25A. Third-Party Inventory — Master register of all vendors and processors sharing personal data: data categories, DPA status, risk rating, onboarding/offboarding checklist, and escalation tracking
  • 33A. Processor Deletion Confirmation — Formal deletion instruction letter to processors, signed confirmation receipt format, sub-processor cascading, tracking log, and overdue escalation protocol
