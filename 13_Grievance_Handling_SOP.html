Grievance Handling SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)
DPDP Act 2023 Section 13 — Data Protection Complaint Resolution & DPBI Escalation
Version: 2.0 (April 2026) | SLA: 48-hour ACK, 30-day resolution, DPBI escalation if unresolved
Authority: DPO owns resolution; DPA acts as escalation final authority

Grievance Intake Process

Step 1: Receipt & Acknowledgement (Within 48 Hours)

What to do:
1. Receive grievance via email (grievance@org.in) or form submission
2. Assign unique reference number (GRV-YYYYMMDD-XXXXX)
3. Log in Grievance Register (see T-30)
4. Send automated ACK email within 48 hours with:
   — Grievance Reference ID
   — Confirmation of complaint receipt
   — Expected resolution date (30 days from submission)
   — DPO contact for questions
5. Notify DPO and relevant department

Grievance SLA Tiers

Grievance Type Definition Resolution SLA Process
Tier 1: Simple Query FAQ-type question, information request, minor clarification 7 days (internal resolution) DPO → Department → Response
Tier 2: Complaint Investigation Allegation of violation, breach disclosure, data misuse claim 30 days (full investigation) DPO → Investigation → Finding → Resolution Letter
Tier 3: DPBI Escalation Unresolved beyond 30 days OR complainant initiates DPBI filing N/A (escalated to DPBI authority) DPO notifies DPBI + provides full case file

DPBI Escalation Workflow

Timeline:
Day 1: Grievance received → ACK sent to complainant
Day 7: Internal resolution attempt (Tier 1) OR investigation kicks off (Tier 2)
Day 30: Final response deadline (Tier 1 resolved OR Tier 2 investigation concludes)
Day 31+: IF UNRESOLVED: DPO informs complainant of DPBI escalation right

DPBI Filing (Complainant's choice):
Complainant may file complaint with Data Protection Board of India (DPBI) at dpb.gov.in (when operational)
DPA will provide all case documents to DPBI within 15 days of DPBI notice

Grievance Tracker Dashboard

Field Purpose
Grievance ID GRV-YYYYMMDD-XXXXX (unique, searchable)
Complainant Name / Email Data Principal who filed grievance
Complaint Date Submission timestamp (triggers SLA)
Complaint Summary 2-3 sentences of complaint nature
Tier Classification Tier 1 (Simple) / Tier 2 (Complaint) / Tier 3 (Escalation)
SLA Due Date 7d (T1) or 30d (T2) from complaint date
Status Open / Under Investigation / Resolved / Escalated to DPBI
Assigned To (DPO/Team) Owner responsible for resolution
Resolution Letter Final response sent date + outcome
SLA Breach Alert Automated alert if not resolved by SLA date

RBI Ombudsman Escalation (NBFC-Specific)

For NBFC organizations:
If grievance relates to NBFC lending/credit services, complainant may also escalate to RBI Ombudsman:
  • RBI Ombudsman: https://rbio.rbi.org.in
  • SLA: Can file if unresolved for 30 days by NBFC
  • Parallel path: Complainant can file with BOTH DPO and RBI Ombudsman
  • Your action: If RBI Ombudsman notice received, escalate immediately to DPA + Legal
