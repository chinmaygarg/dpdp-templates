Legal Basis: Under Section 8(2) and Section 8(7) of the DPDP Act 2023, a Data Fiduciary must instruct its Data Processors to delete personal data once the purpose of processing is no longer served. This template provides: (A) the formal deletion instruction to the processor, (B) the processor's confirmation receipt format, and (C) the tracking log for all deletion confirmations. Failure to obtain processor deletion confirmation within the specified timeline constitutes a compliance gap.

PART D — ESCALATION PROTOCOL (Overdue Confirmations)
ESCALATION TRIGGER: If a signed deletion confirmation receipt is not received within the deadline specified in Part A:
  1. Day 1 after deadline: DPO sends formal reminder to Processor's DPO / Legal contact via email + certified post.
  2. Day 7 after deadline: DPO escalates to CISO and Legal. Consider suspending any new data sharing with this processor pending resolution.
  3. Day 14 after deadline: Legal issues formal breach-of-DPA notice. Log non-compliance in Third-Party Inventory risk rating. Consider whether a breach notification to the Data Protection Board is required under Section 8(6).
  4. Day 30 after deadline: Terminate DPA and remove processor from approved vendor list. Engage legal counsel for liability assessment.
