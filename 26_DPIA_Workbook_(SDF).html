Overview & Purpose

Under DPDP Act 2023 Section 10, every Significant Data Fiduciary (SDF) must conduct a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) before implementing processing activities that pose a high risk to data principals. This Workbook provides a structured 6-step process to identify DPIA-triggering activities, assess risks, implement safeguards, and obtain organizational sign-off before deployment.

Who Must Use This Workbook: If your organization is classified as a Significant Data Fiduciary (SDF) — typically organizations processing >50 lakh personal data records or processing sensitive data at scale — you MUST complete a DPIA for any processing activity listed in the "DPIA Trigger Checklist" below before the activity goes live.

SECTION 2 — 6-STEP DPIA PROCESS

Once you've identified a DPIA-triggering activity, follow the 6-step process below to assess risks and implement safeguards.

STEP 1: Activity Description & Scope

Objective: Clearly document what processing activity you plan to implement and who it affects.

Fill in the following:

  • Processing Activity Name: ___________________________________________________
  • Description: What exactly will the system/process do? [E.g., "AI-based credit scoring for personal loan applications"]
  • Data Categories Involved: [E.g., "Income, employment, transaction history, credit score"]
  • Individuals Affected: How many data principals? [E.g., "All loan applicants; estimated 5,000/month"]
  • Processing Duration: Temporary (one-time) or ongoing? [E.g., "Ongoing; deployed for 3+ years"]
  • Responsible Team: Which business unit owns this? [E.g., "Credit Risk Team, IT Systems Team"]

STEP 2: Identify Legitimate Uses & Legal Basis

Objective: Confirm that this processing activity is justified under DPDP Act Section 6 (consent) or Section 7 (legitimate use).

Complete:

  • ☐ Section 6 Consent: Is consent required? If YES, is consent form (T-08) FSIUU-valid? [Reference T-08 Consent Form]
  • ☐ Section 7 Legitimate Use: Is this justified under Section 7(i-ix)? If YES, which ground? [Reference T-06 Section 7 Memo]
  • ☐ Data Minimization: Is only necessary data being collected? [Justify which data is necessary]
  • ☐ Purpose Limitation: Can data only be used for stated purpose? [List permitted uses]

Risk Score Interpretation:
• Critical (>20): CANNOT proceed without additional safeguards. Implement controls, re-assess risk, obtain board approval.
• High (15-20): High risk. Implement strong controls from "Mitigation" column. DPO sign-off required.
• Medium (10-14): Moderate risk. Standard controls sufficient. Compliance approval required.
• Low (<10): Low risk. Proceed with standard safeguards.
