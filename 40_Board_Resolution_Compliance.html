BOARD RESOLUTION — DIGITAL PERSONAL DATA PROTECTION ACT 2023 COMPLIANCE PROGRAM
Resolution No:
Date:
Adopted at: Board of Directors Meeting held on
RECITALS
WHEREAS, the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023 (hereinafter referred to as "the Act") was passed by Parliament and came into force on August 4, 2023, establishing comprehensive data protection requirements for all organizations processing personal data of Indian residents;
WHEREAS, the DPDP Rules, 2025 were notified on November 14, 2025, providing detailed operational guidelines and framework for implementation of the Act;
WHEREAS, the mandatory compliance deadline is May 13, 2027, by which all organizations must demonstrate full substantive compliance with all applicable DPDP requirements;
WHEREAS, is classified as a under the Act and is therefore subject to mandatory compliance with DPDP Sections 5–16;
WHEREAS, the Board has been briefed on the organization's current compliance posture, identified gaps, and the scope of work required to achieve full compliance;
WHEREAS, the Board believes that establishing a comprehensive, well-resourced, and time-bound DPDP compliance program is essential to (a) fulfill legal obligations, (b) mitigate penalty exposure (up to ₹250 Crore), (c) protect the organization's reputation, and (d) build customer trust;
NOW THEREFORE, IT IS RESOLVED:
1. APPROVAL OF DPDP COMPLIANCE PROGRAM
The Board approves the establishment of a comprehensive Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 Compliance Program (hereinafter "the Compliance Program"), designed to achieve full compliance with all DPDP Act and Rules requirements by May 13, 2027.
2. APPOINTMENT OF DATA PROTECTION OFFICER (SDF Only)
If the organization is a Significant Data Fiduciary (SDF), the Board appoints as the Data Protection Officer (DPO) for a term of .

The DPO shall be:
  • Directly accountable to the Board or Board-delegated committee
  • Independent and free from organizational pressure
  • Responsible for overseeing DPDP compliance program execution
  • Authorized to escalate compliance concerns directly to the Board
3. APPOINTMENT OF PRIVACY / DATA PROTECTION COMMITTEE
The Board establishes a Privacy / Data Protection Committee with the following authority and composition:
Position Member Expertise Area
Chair Strategy / Legal
DPO (if SDF) Data Protection
Chief Information Officer Technology & Security
General Counsel Legal Compliance
Chief Product Officer Product & Operations

The Committee shall report to the Board on compliance status, findings, and recommendations.
4. BUDGET & RESOURCE ALLOCATION
The Board approves allocation of for the Compliance Program, covering:
  • Technology infrastructure (database, audit logging, encryption)
  • Personnel (DPO, compliance team, training)
  • Third-party services (external audit, legal counsel, consultancy)
  • Tools and platforms (consent management, DSAR, DPIA, compliance monitoring)
  • Training and awareness programs
The Chief Financial Officer is authorized to release funds in accordance with an agreed phased disbursement schedule.
5. DELEGATION OF AUTHORITY
The Board delegates to the authority to:
  • Approve detailed Compliance Program execution plan and timeline
  • Enter into contracts with third-party consultants, auditors, and vendors
  • Make tactical decisions on compliance implementation
  • Approve privacy policies, notices, consent mechanisms, and DPAs
  • Establish and approve the Data Protection Officer's charter (if SDF)
All major decisions remain subject to Board oversight and escalation protocols.
6. COMPLIANCE AUDIT & CERTIFICATION
The Board directs that:
  • An internal compliance audit be conducted against the 17-domain DPDP checklist by
  • For SDF organizations, an independent external audit be commissioned by
  • All CRITICAL and HIGH findings be remediated before
  • The organization obtain a Compliance Opinion Letter from the external auditor by
7. ONGOING COMPLIANCE OBLIGATIONS
The Board acknowledges and directs that the organization shall maintain:
  • Annual DPIA review for high-risk processing (SDF)
  • Annual compliance audit (SDF)
  • Quarterly Data Protection Committee meetings
  • Semi-annual Board reporting on compliance status
  • Continuous monitoring of regulatory changes and guidance from the Data Protection Board of India
  • Staff training and awareness programs (annually)
8. REGULATORY REPORTING
The Board directs that:
  • The organization establish and maintain a grievance redressal mechanism as required by DPDP Act Section 13
  • The Data Protection Board of India be notified of the DPO appointment (if SDF) within 30 days of appointment
  • All significant breaches be reported to the DPB within 72 hours (or 6 hours for cyber incidents per CERT-In rules)
  • Annual audit reports be submitted to the Board and retained for 3 years
9. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST & INDEPENDENCE
The Data Protection Officer (if SDF) and any external auditors engaged must:
  • Maintain independence from operational management
  • Disclose any conflicts of interest to the Board
  • Have unrestricted access to Board members and audit findings
  • Not be subject to punitive action for escalations or adverse findings
10. MONITORING & GOVERNANCE
The Board shall:
  • Receive quarterly compliance status reports from the Data Protection Committee
  • Review and approve all compliance audit findings and remediation plans
  • Monitor progress against the compliance roadmap (Phases 1-5, target completion by May 13, 2027)
  • Escalate any CRITICAL compliance gap or regulatory action to the full Board within 5 business days
11. ACKNOWLEDGEMENT OF RESPONSIBILITY
The Board acknowledges and accepts:
  • Full responsibility for DPDP compliance of the organization
  • The penalty risk exposure in case of non-compliance (up to ₹250 Crore per violation, cumulatively)
  • The need for adequate resourcing and management support for the Compliance Program
  • The organization's ongoing obligation to maintain compliance beyond May 13, 2027
