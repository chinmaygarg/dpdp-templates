REGULATORY CHANGE MANAGEMENT — 10-Day Trigger Process

Whenever a new regulation, RBI directive, DPDP Rules clarification, or government guideline is published that affects data processing, the organization must trigger a 10-day regulatory change review. This process ensures Privacy Notices, Consent Forms, policies, and operations are updated to reflect new requirements.

Event Type (Trigger) Examples SLA Update Required Owner & Process
New DPDP Rules / Notification DPDP Data Protection Rules 2025 published; Data Protection Board issues guidance; Government announces DPIA trigger threshold change 10 DAYS Policies, Privacy Notice (T-07), Consent Form (T-08), DPIA triggers (T-26) Legal + DPO (SDF): Legal drafts update by Day 5, DPO reviews by Day 8, deployed by Day 10
RBI / Sector Regulator Change RBI Master Direction update (KYC/AML, retention period), SEBI/IRDAI/ED circular on data handling, new compliance requirement 10 DAYS Data Retention Schedule (T-02A), processor requirements, breach SLA, cross-border rules Compliance Officer + Legal: Assess impact (Day 1), update procedures (Days 2-5), DPO review (Days 6-8), rollout (Days 9-10)
Court Judgment Affecting DPDP High Court rules on validity of consent, data deletion, cross-border transfer, or SDF definition; Supreme Court issues guidance 10 DAYS Legal interpretation memo, consent process, deletion SOP, governance policies Legal Counsel: Opinion letter by Day 5, operational guidance by Day 8, Board notification by Day 10
Critical Breach or Incident Pattern Multiple organizations report similar breach vector (e.g., ransomware targeting fintech); CERT-In issues alert; new vulnerability published affecting DPDP compliance 5 DAYS
(expedited)		 Breach response procedures (T-20), security controls, incident reporting, customer notification CISO + DPO: Risk assessment (Day 1), control update (Days 2-3), testing (Day 4), rollout (Day 5)

Regulatory Change 10-Day Workflow

  1. Day 1 — Detection & Triage: Legal/DPO monitors for new regulations. Upon detection, send alert: "New RBI Master Direction published [date]. Affects [scope: KYC retention, security, etc.]"
  2. Days 2-5 — Impact Assessment & Drafting: Responsible team assesses impact. Which templates, policies, procedures must change? Draft updated versions of Privacy Notice, Consent Form, Data Retention Schedule, or operational procedures.
  3. Days 6-8 — Review & Approval: DPO reviews changes for compliance. Legal counsel verifies interpretation. If SDF, Board Privacy Committee consulted. If disagreement: escalate, document, extend deadline with Board approval.
  4. Days 9-10 — Deploy & Notify: Update Privacy Notice version log (T-07). Redeploy Consent Form. Update relevant policies. Notify all staff via training or email. Send customer notification if material change (e.g., new retention period).
  5. Post-Deployment: Document change in Regulatory Change Log (below). Update 92-Checkpoint tracker to reflect any new compliance items. Include in next Board Privacy Committee report.

DPO QUARTERLY 92-CHECKPOINT REVIEW SCHEDULE

The DPO (for SDF) or Compliance Lead (for DF) conducts quarterly reviews of the 92-checkpoint compliance register. Each quarter focuses on different checkpoints to ensure comprehensive annual coverage. This process identifies gaps early and tracks remediation.

Quarterly Review Meeting Cadence:
• Q1 (31 Mar): DPO presents full audit report + DPIA status
• Q2 (30 Jun): Compliance officer presents consent health metrics + withdrawal analysis
• Q3 (30 Sep): Vendor manager presents DPA/processor audit + risk update
• Q4 (31 Dec): DPO presents annual audit findings + 2025 compliance plan

Escalation: If any quarterly review identifies a critical gap (compliance <60%, >5% invalid consents, DPA gap, vendor >100 risk), immediate DPO alert to Board Chair + CEO. Escalated items require remediation plan within 14 days.
