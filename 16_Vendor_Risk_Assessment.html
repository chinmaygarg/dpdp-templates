Vendor Risk Assessment (VRA)
DPDP Compliance Evaluation + CPaaS Purpose-Level Controls
Version: 2.0 (April 2026) | When: Before engagement + annual renewal
Scope: All third-party vendors with access to personal data (processors, CPaaS providers, cloud providers, BPOs)

Vendor DPDP Compliance Questionnaire (20 Questions)

  1. Do you have a Data Protection Policy compliant with DPDP 2023?
  2. What security controls are in place? (encryption, MFA, audit logging)
  3. Where is personal data stored? (geographic location, cloud region)
  4. Do you sub-process data? If yes, provide list of sub-processors.
  5. What is your annual breach incident history?
  6. When was your last security audit or penetration test?
  7. Do you have ISO 27001 or SOC2 Type II certification?
  8. Can you provide proof-of-deletion on contract termination?
  9. What is your DSAR response time SLA?
  10. Do you support withdrawal propagation within 24 hours?
  11. Is your data processing compliant with cross-border transfer rules? (Section 16, Schedule 1)
  12. Do you have cyber insurance coverage?
  13. What is your incident response time SLA?
  14. Do you comply with RBI/SEBI/IRDAI guidelines (if applicable)?
  15. Can you provide annual audit report on data handling?
  16. Do you have DPO or Privacy Officer on staff?
  17. Can you support individual consent tracking per purpose?
  18. What is your data retention and deletion schedule?
  19. Do you have Cyber Security audit ready for review?
  20. Will you commit to DPBI compliance and audit cooperation?

CPaaS Vendor DPDP Configuration Checklist

For Communication Platform as a Service (CPaaS) vendors (SMS, Email, WhatsApp, Push):

CPaaS Control Required? Verification
Suppression List Sync ≤24h ✓ YES Test withdrawal → confirm suppressed within 24h
Per-Purpose Opt-Out ✓ YES User can unsubscribe from marketing without affecting transactional
Withdrawal Confirmation Mechanism ✓ YES CPaaS sends confirmation callback to Fiduciary's API
No Marketing to Opted-Out ✓ YES Audit CPaaS logs: zero marketing sends post-withdrawal
Separate Consent for WhatsApp vs SMS vs Email ✓ YES User can consent to SMS only, not WhatsApp
DPA Executed with CPaaS ✓ YES Signed DPA on file (see T-15)

Annual Review Process

Every 12 months for each vendor:
1. Send updated DPDP Compliance Questionnaire
2. Request latest security audit / certification (ISO 27001, SOC2)
3. Review incident history (breaches, downtime)
4. Check DPA expiry and renewal status
5. Test CPaaS controls (suppression sync, withdrawal propagation)
6. Assign updated Risk Score
7. Update Vendor Risk Register
8. If Risk Score > 70: escalate to Procurement for renegotiation/replacement
9. Report summary to Board Risk Committee (quarterly)

Implementation Checklist

  • ☐ Identify all vendors with personal data access
  • ☐ Classify vendors by risk level (High/Medium/Low)
  • ☐ Distribute DPDP Compliance Questionnaire
  • ☐ Execute or renew DPAs (see T-15)
  • ☐ For CPaaS vendors: validate suppression list sync + withdrawal propagation
  • ☐ Populate Vendor Risk Register
  • ☐ Schedule annual VRA reviews
  • ☐ Document risk scores and decisions
  • ☐ Report summary to Board quarterly
