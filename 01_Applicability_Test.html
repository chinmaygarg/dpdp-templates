DPDP Applicability Test
Determine if Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 applies to your organization
Purpose: This test determines whether your organization is subject to DPDP Act 2023. DPDP applies when ALL THREE conditions below are met.

Step 1: Three-Part Applicability Test

Answer the three questions below. If ALL are "YES", DPDP applies.

Question 1: Is the data DIGITAL?

Is the personal data collected digitally OR digitized after collection (e.g., scanned, uploaded, entered into a system)?

YES — Data is digital
NO — Only physical data (paper, hardcopy only)

Question 2: Is the data of INDIAN RESIDENTS?

Is the personal data about residents of India (as defined in DPDP as "Data Principals")?

YES — About Indian residents
NO — Only about foreign residents

Question 3: Is processing IN or FOR India?

Does processing occur within India OR are goods/services offered to Indian residents (even if processing happens abroad)?

YES — Processing in India or serving Indian residents
NO — Entirely outside India, no Indian customers

APPLICABILITY DECISION

If all three are YES: DPDP APPLIES
If any is NO: DPDP MAY NOT APPLY

Step 2: If DPDP Applies — Entity Classification

Next: Determine whether your organization is a DF or SDF (see table below)
Classification Definition Obligations
Data Fiduciary (DF) Any organization processing DPDP-covered personal data Sections 5–14 (core compliance: notice, consent, DSAR, security, breach notification, etc.)
Significant Data Fiduciary (SDF) Designated by Central Government (criteria: 10M+ users, sensitive data at scale, national security impact) +Section 10 (DPO appointment, DPIA, annual independent audit)
SDF Status: Government has not yet formally designated organizations as SDF. If you process data of 10M+ users or sensitive data at national scale, monitor Government notifications for designation after Nov 14, 2025.

Step 3: Industry Overlay Assessment

Important: DPDP compliance must also satisfy regulator-specific requirements. Check which apply to your industry.
Regulator Applies To Key DPDP Overlap Stricter Requirement
RBI Banks, NBFCs, Fintech Data localization (India-only) 8-year retention (loan data)
PMLA Financial institutions KYC record retention 5 years post-relationship
CERT-In ALL organizations (cybersecurity) Breach notification timeline 6 hours (vs DPDP 72 hrs — 6 hrs applies)
IRDAI Insurance companies Policy data retention Per Insurance Act
SEBI Brokers, asset managers, securities firms Trade records 5-year retention

Decision Summary

Scenario Action
YES to all 3 + no industry overlay → DPDP applies as DF. Proceed to Phase 1 Assessment.
YES to all 3 + has industry overlay → DPDP applies as DF. Must also comply with regulator requirements. Use stricter deadlines/retention periods.
YES to all 3 + likely SDF → DPDP applies. Plan for SDF obligations (Section 10): DPO, DPIA, annual audit. Monitor Government notifications.
NO to any question → DPDP may not apply. Confirm with legal counsel. Check state privacy laws as fallback.

Next Steps

  1. Complete all three questions above
  2. If all YES: Classify organization as DF or SDF (see Step 2)
  3. Identify any industry overlays that apply (see Step 3)
  4. Prepare engagement kickoff with Phase 1 (Assessment) timeline
  5. Document applicability decision for compliance records
