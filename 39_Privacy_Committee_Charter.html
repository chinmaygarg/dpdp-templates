Data Protection / Privacy Committee Charter
Effective Date:
1. PURPOSE & AUTHORITY

This Charter establishes the Data Protection / Privacy Committee (the "Committee") for to oversee and ensure compliance with the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act 2023, DPDP Rules 2025, and applicable privacy regulations.

Authority Source: Board of Directors Resolution dated

2. SCOPE & RESPONSIBILITIES

The Committee shall have authority to:

  • Oversee DPDP compliance program design, implementation, and continuous improvement
  • Review and approve data protection policies, privacy notices, and consent mechanisms
  • Receive and review compliance audit reports and gap analyses
  • Monitor and track DPDP-related incidents, breaches, and regulatory actions
  • Ensure data subject rights (DSAR, withdrawal, grievance) are operationalized and monitored
  • Review and approve third-party Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)
  • Oversee Data Protection Officer (DPO) performance and independence (SDF organizations)
  • Review and approve Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) for high-risk processing (SDF)
  • Monitor compliance with Section 16 cross-border transfer restrictions
  • Ensure training and awareness programs are in place
  • Report compliance status to the Board at least

Quorum:

  • Approve DPIA scope and conclusions (Committee, with DPO sign-off for SDF)

6. AGENDA SETTING & MATERIALS
  • Agenda: Prepared by Chair and DPO, distributed 5 business days in advance
  • Materials: Compliance reports, audit findings, policy drafts, incident summaries
  • Confidentiality: All Committee materials are confidential and shall be marked "Legal Privilege"
  • Minutes: Recorded and retained for 5 years, signed by Chair
7. ESCALATION PROTOCOL

CRITICAL Findings (escalate to Board within 10 days):

  • Confirmed data breach affecting >100 principals or sensitive data
  • Failure to meet regulatory notification deadlines (DPB 72h, CERT-In 6h)
  • Non-compliance by SDF with Section 10 obligations (DPO, DPIA, audit)
  • Third-party processor failure to meet contractual obligations
  • Regulatory enforcement action or penalty notice from DPB

HIGH Priority (escalate within 30 days):

  • Gaps in consent registry or ROPA completeness
  • Delays in DSAR responses (approaching 90-day SLA)
  • Data retention policy violations or ineffective deletion
  • Security vulnerabilities requiring remediation
8. CONFLICT OF INTEREST
  • Committee members shall disclose any actual or perceived conflicts of interest
  • Member involved in matter under review may be asked to recuse themselves
  • Conflicts logged in meeting minutes
9. CHARTER AMENDMENT

This Charter may be amended by Board resolution. Amendments effective upon Board approval.
