1. Purpose & Legal Basis

Under Section 14 of the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, every Data Principal has the right to nominate another person who shall, in the event of the Data Principal's death or incapacity, represent the Data Principal for the purposes specified in Section 11(4).

This form allows Data Principals to designate a nominee who can exercise their rights under the DPDP Act on their behalf.

2. Definitions

Term Definition
Data Principal An individual who provides personal data, or on whose behalf the data is provided
Nominee A person nominated by the Data Principal to represent them in the event of death or incapacity
Incapacitated Unable to make decisions due to physical or mental condition

4. Scope of Nomination

The nominee is authorized to exercise the following rights on behalf of the Data Principal:

  • Right to Access (DSAR): Request and receive copies of personal data held by the Data Fiduciary
  • Right to Correction: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of personal data (subject to legal obligations)
  • Right to Grievance: Lodge complaints with the Data Fiduciary or Data Protection Board
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent for processing of personal data
  • Right to Transfer: Request transfer of personal data to another Data Fiduciary

5. Conditions & Limitations

  • The nominee can only act in the event of the Data Principal's death or incapacity
  • The nominee cannot further delegate this authority to another person
  • The Data Principal may revoke or change the nomination at any time
  • The nomination is automatically revoked if the nominee predeceases the Data Principal
  • The Data Fiduciary may verify the nominee's identity before acting on their requests
  • The nominee must provide proof of the Data Principal's death or incapacity
