DPDP Act 2023 — Section 9 (Age <18 Data Processing)
DPDP Section: Section 9 (Children's Data Protection) | Version: 1.0 | Effective Date: [DATE]
Scope: ANY processing of data of individuals under 18 years of age | Compliance Deadline: 13 May 2027

Overview

Section 9 of the DPDP Act 2023 imposes STRICT rules on processing children's personal data:

⚠️ Definition of Child: ANY individual below 18 years of age. No exceptions.

This policy sets out how [Organization] complies with Section 9 requirements when processing data of users, customers, beneficiaries, or employees who are children (age < 18).

FinTech/BFSI Relevance: Student loan platforms, junior savings accounts, teen investment apps, child insurance products, and payment apps used by minors must all comply with Section 9. This policy is MANDATORY if your fintech serves any users under 18.
---

Section 9 Requirements Summary

Requirement What It Means Impact
Section 9(1): Parental Consent MUST obtain verifiable consent of parent/lawful guardian BEFORE processing child's data MANDATORY
Section 9(2): Age Verification MUST verify the user is actually a child before applying Section 9 rules MANDATORY
Section 9(3): Prohibited Processing MUST NOT do tracking, profiling, behavioral monitoring, targeted advertising, or processing that harms child well-being ABSOLUTE BAN
Section 9(4): Exemptions Schools, hospitals, certain transport providers EXEMPT from (1) & (3) rules under strict conditions Does not apply to fintech

Step 2: Age Verification Mechanism

Section 9(2) requires age verification BEFORE you can confirm if Section 9 rules apply. You must verify whether the user is actually under 18.

POLICY: Age Verification at Onboarding

When a new user signs up, [Organization] will:

  • Method 1 (Recommended): Ask for date of birth → Calculate age → If <18, trigger parental consent flow
  • Method 2: Aadhaar verification via UIDAI API → Age auto-determined
  • Method 3: Government ID scan → Manual age verification
  • Method 4: School email verification (@school.edu.in) → Assume age <18

Selected Method for [Organization]: [Method 1 / 2 / 3 / 4 / Combination]

FinTech Example: A student loan platform must verify age at signup using method 2 (Aadhaar) or method 4 (school email). If age = 18+, normal consent applies. If age <18, parental consent required.

Step 4: ABSOLUTE PROHIBITIONS Under Section 9(3)

Section 9(3) is an ABSOLUTE BAN. Even with parental consent, these activities are FORBIDDEN:

🚫 PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES (Even with Parental Consent):

1. Tracking

  • ❌ GPS location tracking (except parental control features initiated by parent)
  • ❌ Device tracking (device ID, IMEI)
  • ❌ App usage tracking (which apps child opens)
  • ❌ Web browsing history
  • ❌ Biometric tracking (fingerprint, face recognition for profiling)

2. Profiling

  • ❌ Building child's behavioral profile (preferences, interests, habits)
  • ❌ Personality scoring (risk profile, credit score calculation for child)
  • ❌ Predictive profiling (what child will do in future)

3. Behavioral Monitoring

  • ❌ Monitoring which websites child visits
  • ❌ Analyzing child's spending patterns (to predict future behavior)
  • ❌ Monitoring social media activity

4. Targeted Advertising

  • ❌ Personalized ads based on child's interests/behavior
  • ❌ Retargeting ads (ads following child across websites)
  • ❌ Dynamic pricing (charging child higher price based on behavior profile)
  • ❌ Behavioral advertising (ads about things child looked at)

Allowed: ✅ Generic, non-personalized ads (same for all users)

5. Processing Detrimental to Child Well-Being (S.9(2))

  • ❌ Exposure to inappropriate content (gambling, adult content, extreme violence)
  • ❌ Psychological harm (deliberate psychological pressure, bullying data)
  • ❌ Harm to development (restricting child's natural development)

POLICY STATEMENT: [Organization] categorically prohibits all Section 9(3) activities for child users. Violation results in immediate suspension and referral to Data Protection Board of India.

Step 5: ALLOWED Processing for Children

Section 9 allows ONLY these limited processing activities:

✅ PERMITTED PROCESSING (For Children):

1. Account Management & Basic Services

  • ✅ Name, DOB, contact details (for account management)
  • ✅ Transaction processing (to execute loan/account services)
  • ✅ Customer support (to respond to child/parent inquiries)
  • ✅ Fraud prevention (non-behavioral: checking for duplicate accounts)

2. Legally Required Processing

  • ✅ KYC compliance (RBI requirement — Aadhaar, address verification)
  • ✅ AML/CFT checks (PMLA requirement — checking against sanctions lists)
  • ✅ Tax reporting (if applicable — PAN for earnings)

3. Parent-Initiated Controls

  • ✅ Parental controls (if parent explicitly requests — e.g., "monitor my child's transactions")
  • ✅ Spending limits (parent sets budget)
  • ✅ Location sharing (only if parent initiates, for safety)

4. Educational/Non-Personalized Content

  • ✅ Financial literacy content (generic, same for all users)
  • ✅ News/updates (non-personalized)
  • ✅ Generic product information

Step 6: FinTech-Specific Use Cases

📱 Use Case 1: Student Savings Account (Age 14-17)

Scenario: 16-year-old student wants to open savings account with [Fintech Platform]

Section 9 Compliance Required:

  • ✅ Age verification: Aadhaar confirms age 16
  • ✅ Parental consent form obtained from parent via SMS OTP + Email
  • ✅ Parent identity verified (Aadhaar)
  • ✅ Child can deposit money, view balance, make transfers (to parent only)
  • ❌ NO tracking of child's spending patterns
  • ❌ NO behavioral ads (generic financial education shown)
  • ❌ NO targeted advertising based on child's usage
  • ✅ KYC data collected per RBI requirement (Aadhaar, address)
  • ✅ Parent can set spending limits or withdraw consent anytime

Compliance Status: ✅ COMPLIANT with Section 9

💰 Use Case 2: Student Loan Platform (Age 18-22)

Scenario: 19-year-old student takes ₹5 lakh education loan

Section 9 Applicability: Age 19 = ADULT. Section 9 does NOT apply (student is 18+). Normal consent process applies.

BUT: If student was 17 when loan disbursed:

  • ✅ Parental consent obtained (for borrower age 17)
  • ✅ Parental guarantor role explicit in loan docs
  • ✅ Monthly EMI payment data collected (account management)
  • ❌ NO behavioral tracking of child's financial health
  • ✅ Default assessment allowed per Section 7(iv) if EMI missed
  • ✅ EMI becomes parental responsibility (guarantor) — parent can be tracked/contacted

Data Retention: Child's data deleted on 18th birthday (unless legal hold applies — e.g., ongoing loan repayment). If loan active, data retained for loan servicing only.

📈 Use Case 3: Teen Investment App (Age <18)

Scenario: Platform wants to launch investment app for minors (ages 13-17)

Section 9 Compliance Requirements:

  • ✅ Parental consent: Parent must explicitly authorize child investments
  • ✅ Age verification: All users confirmed <18 via Aadhaar
  • ❌ FORBIDDEN: "Suggest investments based on teen's behavior" (profiling ban)
  • ❌ FORBIDDEN: "Show ads for growth stocks to aggressive traders" (behavioral ads ban)
  • ✅ ALLOWED: Non-personalized financial education (same for all users)
  • ✅ ALLOWED: Portfolio holdings (necessary for account management)
  • ✅ ALLOWED: Fraud detection (non-behavioral checks — duplicate accounts)

Recommended Features for Section 9 Compliance:

  • • Investment limits (e.g., max ₹1000/month) — safeguard against harm
  • • Parent co-approval required for each investment
  • • No margin/leverage trading for minors
  • • No derivatives/options (too risky for minors)
  • • Educational content on investment basics (non-personalized)

Compliance Status: ✅ COMPLIANT if above safeguards implemented

Step 7: Data Retention & Auto-Deletion

Children's data CANNOT be retained indefinitely. When processing stops:

POLICY: Data Retention for Children

Scenario 1: Child Reaches Age 18

  • Automatic review triggered on child's 18th birthday
  • If no active service (no account, no loan): DELETE all child data within 30 days
  • If active service (ongoing loan/account): Transition to adult data retention policy (6-7 years per RBI/PMLA)
  • Send parent/child notification: "We are transitioning to adult data processing. Your data will be retained per [policy]"

Scenario 2: Parent Withdraws Consent

  • Immediately cease processing child's data
  • Delete within 30 days (subject to legal holds)
  • Examples of legal holds: ongoing loan repayment, court order, police investigation

Scenario 3: Child Account Closed

  • Delete transaction data within 90 days
  • Retain only: KYC data (RBI requirement — 7 years), tax records (IT Act — 6 years)
  • No other uses (no marketing, no analytics)

Retention Schedule (Default):

Data Type While Child <18 After Age 18 (No Active Service)
Account info (name, email, phone) Until consent withdrawn or account closed Delete within 30 days
KYC data (Aadhaar, address) Until account closed Retain 7 years (RBI requirement)
Transaction data Until account closed Delete within 90 days
Loan/repayment data Until loan closed Retain 7 years (statutory hold)

Step 8: Harm Assessment Framework

Before launching ANY product/feature for children, conduct a Harm Assessment:

TEMPLATE: Children's Data Processing Harm Assessment

1. Describe the Processing:

What data will be collected and how will it be used? E.g., "Collect spending data to recommend investment products"

2. Identify Potential Harms:

  • Could this processing harm the child's physical, mental, or social well-being?
  • Examples: Loss of privacy, psychological pressure, financial loss, exposure to inappropriate content

3. Risk Assessment:

  • Probability of harm: [High / Medium / Low]
  • Severity of harm: [Severe / Moderate / Minor]
  • Overall risk: [Unacceptable / High / Medium / Low]

4. Mitigation Measures:

  • How will you eliminate/reduce the harm?
  • Examples: Investment limits, parent approval required, no algorithmic decision-making

5. Conclusion:

Is this processing [APPROVED / APPROVED with conditions / REJECTED] under Section 9?

Example: Investment App

  • Processing: Collect spending data to recommend stocks
  • Harm: Child loses savings due to risky investment (Severe)
  • Risk: High (if no safeguards)
  • Mitigation: Investment limit ₹1000/month + parent approval + no margin trading
  • Result: ✅ APPROVED with conditions

Compliance Checklist

Before launching ANY feature for children:

  • ☐ Determined that your organization processes children's data
  • ☐ Age verification mechanism in place and tested
  • ☐ Parental consent form created (DPDP-compliant, in local language)
  • ☐ Parent identity & legal relationship verification process defined
  • ☐ ALL Section 9(3) prohibitions documented in policy (no exceptions)
  • ☐ Only allowed processing activities explicitly listed
  • ☐ Harm assessment completed for each child-facing feature
  • ☐ Data retention schedule defined (with auto-deletion on age 18)
  • ☐ Parental controls implemented (withdraw consent, set limits)
  • ☐ Child data encryption enabled (at rest & in transit)
  • ☐ Privacy notice updated to disclose Section 9 rules
  • ☐ Training provided to all staff on Section 9 prohibitions
  • ☐ Annual audit of child data processing conducted

⚠️ PENALTIES FOR NON-COMPLIANCE: Violations of Section 9 attract penalties up to ₹200 Crore under DPDP Act. Processing children's data without proper safeguards is a high-risk compliance failure.

References & Exemptions

Organizations EXEMPT from Section 9(1) & (3) (with conditions):

  • Educational institutions (schools, colleges) — if processing for educational purposes only
  • Clinical/mental health establishments — if processing for health purposes only
  • Transport providers engaged by schools — if processing for transport safety only

Not applicable to FinTech/BFSI — Fintech is NOT exempt.
