1. Purpose & Applicability (DPDP Section 10 — SDF Only)

Requirement: DPDP Act Section 10 mandates that Significant Data Fiduciaries (SDF) conduct annual compliance audits by independent auditors and publish findings to the Data Protection Board (DPB).

SDF Criteria (DPDP Rules 2025): Organization processing >1M principals' data, OR processing sensitive categories (Aadhaar, health, financial), OR government agency, OR deemed significant by DPB.

Output: Annual Audit Report published by 31 March of following fiscal year; Board notification + DPB submission.

Independence: Auditor must be independent of data fiduciary governance; DPO cannot be lead auditor; Big 4 audit firm or DPDP-registered auditor mandatory.

2. Audit Opinion Framework (4 Types)

Opinion Type Definition Conditions DPB Notification
Unqualified Organization in full compliance with DPDP ≥97% compliance score, no CRITICAL findings, all prior-year findings remediated Green flag — no action required
Qualified Compliance achieved with noted exceptions 80-96% compliance score, 1-3 HIGH findings with documented remediation plans, or CRITICAL remediated <30 days Yellow flag — monitor remediation progress quarterly
Adverse Material non-compliance with DPDP <80% compliance score, 4+ HIGH findings, 1+ CRITICAL remaining unresolved, missed SLA on 10+ data principal rights Red flag — DPB investigation may be triggered; enforcement action possible
Disclaimer Auditor unable to form opinion due to scope limitations Incomplete access to records, inability to validate key systems, legal holds preventing audit Red flag — DPB may suspend operations until scope limitations resolved

3. Annual Report Structure (8 Sections)

Section 1: Executive Summary (1 page)
Audit opinion (unqualified/qualified/adverse/disclaimer), compliance score [X]%, prior-year findings remediation status (100%/N%), critical incidents this year (count), key recommendations.

Section 2: Auditor Independence Statement (0.5 page)
Auditor firm name + credentials, independence confirmation (no conflicts, no financial ties to auditee), audit scope (92 checkpoints, 17 domains, [N] days spent), audit methodology (document review, system testing, control walkthroughs).

Section 3: Prior-Year Findings Status (1 page)
Comparison table: finding ID | prior-year severity | current status (Remediated/In Progress/Not Remediated/New) | evidence of closure | revised timeline if still open.

Section 4: Current-Year Findings (2-3 pages)
Detailed gap register: Finding ID | Domain | Description | Severity (C/H/M/L) | Root cause | Remediation plan | Target date | Owner. Findings sorted by severity. Evidence photos/logs attached in appendix.

Section 5: Control Effectiveness Assessment (1 page)
Per-domain risk rating change vs prior year (↑ improved, ↓ degraded, → same). 17-domain heat map with prior and current status. Trend analysis (compliance score trend over 3 years if available).

Section 6: Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) Summary (1 page, SDF-only)
Count of DPIA-triggering projects completed this year, list of high-risk activities identified, remediation actions taken, DPIA register URL.

Section 7: Incident & Breach Summary (1 page)
Count of breaches reported to DPB, CERT-In, principals | average response time vs 72-hour SLA | customer notification compliance | root cause patterns (human error / misconfiguration / external threat).

Section 8: Auditor Recommendations to Board Privacy Committee (1 page)
Top 3-5 priorities for next 12 months based on findings | resource needs (budget, headcount, tools) | risk areas to monitor | regulatory changes anticipated | board decision required (e.g., approve remediation timeline).

5. DPB Submission & Publication Requirements

Mandatory DPB Submission Deadline: 31 March (fiscal year end + 3 months for audit completion)

Report Format: PDF + digital signature by auditor + DPO
DPB Portal: DPB website (dpb.gov.in — expected 2025/2026) or via registered portal
Public Disclosure: Organization must publish summary of findings on website within 30 days of DPB submission (not full report if unqualified; qualified/adverse must disclose mitigation timeline)
Retention: Auditor + DPO retain full working papers 7 years; accessible to DPB on demand

Failure to Submit: DPB may impose ₹5-10 Cr penalty + suspend data processing pending audit completion + publication
