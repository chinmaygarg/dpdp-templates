5. Detailed Findings & Remediation Roadmap

Severity Classification:
CRITICAL (₹50–100 Cr penalty) — Missing mandatory controls → 30-day SLA
HIGH (₹25–50 Cr penalty) — Incomplete implementation → 60-day SLA
MEDIUM (₹10–25 Cr penalty) — Minor gaps → 90-day SLA
LOW (<₹10 Cr) — Process improvement → No deadline

HIGH Priority Findings

FINDING H-001: Automated Breach Detection & Alerting
Domain: Breach Management (Section 8(6))
Description: Breach incidents currently detected via manual logs. No automated alerting for unauthorized access, bulk exports, or privilege escalation. While breach notification procedure exists (72h to DPB), early detection capability is missing.
Severity: HIGH (₹25–50 Cr)
SLA: 60 days from audit date
Recommendation: Deploy SIEM (Security Information & Event Management) with real-time alerting for data exfiltration, failed authentication (>5 attempts), privilege escalation, mass data access. Integrate with incident response workflow.
Status: ⏳ In Progress (Implementation Q2)
FINDING H-002: Consent Versioning & Retrospective Re-consent
Domain: Consent & Notice (Section 6)
Description: ~12,000 consents from pre-DPDP era lack version tracking. While current consent forms are FSIUU-compliant, retrospective notice and re-consent campaign for legacy principals is pending (Section 5(2) compliance).
Severity: HIGH (₹25–50 Cr)
SLA: 60 days from audit date
Recommendation: Launch retrospective notice campaign (email + SMS) explaining new rights, requesting re-consent per purpose. Audit sample of 10% (1,200 records) post-campaign to verify FSIUU compliance. Track completion rate.
Status: ⏳ Planned (Campaign Q2)

MEDIUM Priority Findings

FINDING M-001: Privacy Notice Content Expansion
Domain: Consent & Notice (Section 6)
Description: Current privacy notice complies with FSIUU but lacks explicit disclosure of automated decision-making (scoring models, risk assessment algorithms). Section 7 guidance recommends transparency on significant automated uses.
Severity: MEDIUM
SLA: 90 days
Recommendation: Enhance privacy notice with: (a) List of automated decision-making activities, (b) Impacts of decisions (loan approval/denial, pricing), (c) Right to human review/appeal, (d) Opt-out availability where feasible.
Status: 📋 Planned (Q3)
FINDING M-002: DPIA Documentation for Profiling Activities
Domain: DPIA & Legitimate Uses (Section 7, Rule 6)
Description: Credit scoring and financial profiling activities are high-risk (risk of discriminatory decisions). DPIA process exists but documentation for these specific activities is incomplete.
Severity: MEDIUM
SLA: 90 days
Recommendation: Complete DPIA workbook for: (a) Credit scoring model, (b) Behavior-based profiling, (c) Fraud detection rules. Document mitigation controls (fairness reviews, bias testing, human override capability).
Status: 📋 Planned (Q3)
FINDING M-003: Consent Manager Integration Audit
Domain: Consent Manager Interoperability (Rules 2025)
Description: Rules 2025 require integration with government-approved Consent Manager list (effective Nov 14, 2026). Current consent system is proprietary; Consent Manager API integration needs testing and audit.
Severity: MEDIUM
SLA: 90 days (before Nov 14, 2026)
Recommendation: (a) Select Consent Manager from approved list, (b) Integrate API for consent propagation/withdrawal, (c) Audit cross-org consent propagation (sample 5%), (d) Test withdrawal cascade to all processors.
Status: 📋 Planned (Q4 2026)

LOW Priority Findings

FINDING L-001: Response SOP Documentation Enhancement
Domain: Data Principal Rights (Section 11–14)
Description: Response workflows exist and are performing well (avg 4.2d, under 30d SLA). Documentation could be enhanced with more detailed step-by-step procedures for edge cases (deceased principals, guardianship, international requests).
Severity: LOW (Process optimization)
Recommendation: Expand SOP with: (a) Deceased principal escalation, (b) Guardianship verification, (c) International request procedures, (d) Conflict resolution workflows.
Status: 💬 Backlog (Post-remediation phase)
