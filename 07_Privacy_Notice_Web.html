4. Your Rights as a Data Principal

Under DPDP Act 2023, you have the following rights:

  • Right to Access: Obtain a copy of your personal data we hold (DSAR)
  • Right to Correction: Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal holds)
  • Right to Grievance: Lodge a complaint with our DPO or Data Protection Board
  • Right to Nomination: Designate a nominee to exercise rights on your behalf
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent for any processing activity anytime

How to Exercise These Rights: Email [privacy@example.com] with your request. We will respond within 90 days.

Privacy Notice Update Workflow

  1. Detect Trigger: Legal team, DPO, or Compliance identifies regulatory change or operational change that affects Privacy Notice
  2. Assess Impact: Is this a 5-day (critical), 10-day (important), 30-day (admin), or annual review trigger? Document in [Org] Privacy Notice Update Tracker (spreadsheet or form)
  3. Draft Update: Legal/Compliance drafts updated section(s). Include change summary for version control log
  4. Review & Approve:
    • 5-day trigger: DPO + Board Privacy Committee review
    • 10-day trigger: DPO + Legal approval
    • 30-day trigger: Legal sign-off (DPO may review)
    • Annual review: DPO certification required
  5. Version Increment: Update version number (e.g., 1.0 → 1.1); enter new row in Version Control Log above
  6. Deploy to Web: Update Privacy Notice HTML on website, mobile app, and all customer-facing channels. Ensure multi-language versions updated simultaneously (22 languages)
  7. Notify Data Principals:
    • 5-day & 10-day triggers: Send email to all registered users within 24 hours of publication
    • Email subject: "[Org Name] Privacy Notice Updated — [Summary of Change]"
    • Include: Link to new notice, link to previous version (archival), explanation of what changed, DPO contact for questions
    • Admin dashboard: Show banner "Privacy Notice updated on [Date]. Review changes here."
  8. Audit Log Entry: Record update in audit trail with: version #, approval date, approver name, deployment date, data principal notification method
  9. DSAR Readiness: If a data principal submits DSAR (right to access), provide them with: (a) current Privacy Notice, (b) all previous versions for 3 years (showing historical processing transparency)
Compliance Reminder (Checkpoint SS-08): Failure to update Privacy Notice within SLA when processing changes = DPDP Section 5(d) accountability violation (₹50Cr penalty exposure). The 5-day and 10-day SLAs are STRICT. Missing SLA requires:
• Immediate escalation to Board Privacy Committee
• Root cause analysis
• Corrective action plan
• Retroactive DSAR review (ensure all 30-day DSAR responses disclosed current practices, not outdated notice)
• Document in next Board compliance report
