FSIUU Framework: 5-Attribute Validity Test

For consent to be legally valid under DPDP Section 6, it MUST satisfy ALL 5 FSIUU criteria. Each is scored (1 point if satisfied, 0 if not). Consent is valid only if score = 5/5.

⚠️ CRITICAL SCORING:
Organizations must verify each criterion and document the score. If score < 5, consent is invalid and processing cannot proceed. This is a mandatory audit requirement per DPBI compliance.

The 5 FSIUU Attributes

Attribute Definition How to Verify Score
F = Free Consent given voluntarily, without pressure, coercion, or threat of service denial • Non-mandatory consent (user can refuse without service loss)
• No pre-ticked boxes (user must actively check)
• No bundled consent (can refuse one purpose without affecting others)		 1 or 0
S = Specific Consent is for a single, named purpose — not a blanket "use my data for anything" • Form lists the exact purpose (e.g., "Marketing", "Loan processing", "Analytics")
• Not vague (e.g., "Business purposes" is too broad)
• Each purpose has separate consent checkbox		 1 or 0
I = Informed User understands what data is collected, how it will be used, who has access, and how long it's kept • Privacy Notice linked or attached
• Data categories listed in form (Name, Email, Phone, etc.)
• Processors/third parties identified
• Data retention period stated
• Evidence user read it (checkbox: "I have read Privacy Notice")		 1 or 0
U = Unconditional Refusing this consent does NOT result in loss of essential services • Non-essential consent (marketing, analytics, profiling)
• If essential (e.g., KYC for loan), must be documented as such
• User explicitly told: "Refusing this consent will NOT affect your service"		 1 or 0
U = Unambiguous Consent is a clear, affirmative choice — not silence, inaction, or pre-ticked box • User MUST actively check the box (not pre-checked)
• User clicks "I Consent" button (affirmative action)
• No opt-in by silence (e.g., "I will contact you unless you object")
• Timestamp of consent captured		 1 or 0

Withdrawal Instructions

You can withdraw consent for ANY purpose at any time, without penalty or loss of essential services.

How to Withdraw Consent

Option 1: Online Portal (Instant)
1. Log into your account at: [PORTAL_URL]
2. Go to Settings → Data & Consent
3. Find the consent you want to withdraw (e.g., "Marketing", "Analytics")
4. Click "Withdraw Consent"
5. Confirmation email sent immediately
6. Processing stops within 24 hours

Option 2: Email (24-hour response)
Send email to: privacy@[organization].com
Subject: "Withdraw Consent — [PURPOSE_NAME]"
Include: Your name, account ID, purpose to withdraw
You'll receive confirmation within 24 hours

Option 3: Phone (48-hour response)
Call: [PHONE_NUMBER]
Say: "I want to withdraw consent for [PURPOSE_NAME]"
We'll send written confirmation within 48 hours

What Happens After Withdrawal

  • ✅ We stop processing your data for that purpose immediately (or within 24 hours)
  • ✅ We notify all processors to stop (T-24 DPA requires them to comply)
  • ✅ Data already processed is retained per legal requirement (RBI, tax, litigation hold), but NOT used for that purpose anymore
  • ✅ You keep all other services (service for other purposes continues)
  • ✅ You can re-consent anytime if you change your mind
⚠️ IMPORTANT:
If you withdraw consent for KYC/loan processing, we must stop processing immediately. This may result in loan cancellation or account closure per contract terms.

Special Cases: CPaaS, LSP, Minors

1. CPaaS Consent (Customer Premises Equipment)

If you're a business customer using our CPaaS services (SMS/Voice):
Your consent is governed by additional regulations. Check the CPaaS Addendum (attached) for:

  • Verification requirements (callback, OTP)
  • Mandatory consent logging for regulatory audit
  • Do-Not-Call (DNC) registry compliance

2. LSP Channel Consent (Licensed Service Provider / Direct Seller)

If you provided consent through a partner (Direct Seller, LSP, etc.):
We must verify that consent was obtained correctly at the source (partner), not just by us asking again.

  • Partner must provide: Consent date, FSIUU verification, partner name, agent ID
  • If verification fails, we cannot process your data until re-consent obtained
  • You can withdraw at either us OR the partner
